Gentoo, the one who knows Linux best

Now I’ve come back to Gentoo again just after trying Fedora 17 for not more than one day. The more familiar I am with Gentoo, the more unfamiliar with other distros will be. Besides this, knowing a distro well is far more than managing packages and services, all the supporting facilities like documents, irc channels, forums, mailinglists, wikis will also take a long time for one to be acquainted with. So to change a Linux distro means a lot to me.

It was at first the ‘compile everything’ working way of Gentoo that made  me mad, but now I realize that this is the very base of every great feature the distro could have. Portage, the package system inspired from FreeBSD Ports, the heart of Gentoo, could give you the abilities to know and control everything in your operating system, and what better than Ports is, in a more uniform, simple, and elegant way.Usually opening every details is not that hard, the real challenge is how to manage all the details simply and uniformly. Gentoo was born with this temperament, or I would rather say, it was born for this. USE, keywords, ebuid, cascading profile, eselect, slot, rc-update, overlay and more, all these are great manners abstracted from the Linux open source ecosystem. Some would say some of theme are much too complex or useless, but people who are experienced with Linux will admit that the true complexities are from Linux itself.  The “bazaar” developing way may produce some great projects, but when collaborating some projects of this kind together as a whole, owning to the lack of standards and cooperation, problem and complexity occurs, that’s why so many Linux distros exist in the world and each other differs a lot.  Among them some  thinks highly of simplicity, some focus on security, some pay more attention on user experiences, but Gentoo believes in “no-doing”, it just gives you all the freedom and serves for every purpose. In the future maybe  standards in the Linux world will be more and more mature and we’ll have more and more standard packages(units) like wayland, systemd etc, things will get better. Even now, Gentoo has already shown its ability to explore every single piece or hold the full shot of Linux world, and once again, in an elegant and uniform way. I would like to proudly say that Gentoo is the one who knows Linux best.

OK. Here are the problems I encountered in this installation and the solutions.

  1. while emergeing "gnome-light",  some blocks(the lower-case ‘b’, means in fact conflicts that could be resolved automatically) occurred, I just unmerged the packages causing the conflicts, including "sys-libs/zlib", then python failed. I had to start from scratch.
  2. I mounted the wrong partition for backing up configuration files(.config etc), when back to the LiveCD environment, the disk with backup files was formatted immediately. Nothing useful left, so I had to configure the kernel once again.
  3. I installed systemd, but it can’t load Gnome3. I checked the wiki, appended the following 2 lines to "/etc/pam.d/system-auth", problem solved.

    session optional pam_loginuid.so
    session optional pam_systemd.so

  4. NetworkManager and nm-applet of Gnome 3 works now, but the usb wifi couldn’t resume from sleep.  I googled and added the following line to "/etc/pm/config.d/unload_modules", problem solved.

    SUSPEND_MODULES="$SUSPEND_MODULES rtl8192cu"

  5. Set up beautiful PS1 in ".bashrc".

    export PS1="\[\033[01;32;44m\] \u \[\033[01;32;41m\] \h \[\033[00m\] [\W] \$ "

 

Now I’ll make a few decisions here:

  1. Do not upgrade Gnome 3 until it and its full dependencies are stable in portage tree.
  2. Do not leave Gentoo again.
